# The Class Story

This file is the running narrative of this class's experiment — the logbook the AI keeps so the semester adds up to a story instead of a pile of chats. At the end of the quarter, this file (plus the teacher's reflections) becomes the end-of-experiment report.

## Rules for the AI

1. **Aggregate only.** Same rule as everywhere in this project: no student names, no per-student data, ever. "4 students moved out of the struggling tier" — never who.
2. **One short entry per week**, written right after the teacher pastes a Class Pulse summary (the Friday ritual in `brain/weekly-rhythm.md`). Three to five sentences: what the numbers showed, what the AI and teacher tried, what happened, what's next.
3. **Append, never rewrite.** Past entries are the record. Fix a factual error if the teacher asks; otherwise leave history alone.
4. **Tell it like a story, not a status report.** This is the document the teacher will reread in June. "The week the missing-work cards finally landed" beats "Tier counts improved."
5. **Monthly and quarterly reflections** (from the weekly rhythm) get their own slightly longer entries, marked as such.
