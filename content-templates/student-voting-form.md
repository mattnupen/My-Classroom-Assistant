# Student Voting Form

*This is the template for the form your students fill out when they help shape the AI. Adapt the questions to fit what you're letting them decide. Put it in Google Forms, Microsoft Forms, or hand it out on paper — whatever your class uses.*

---

## What to share with students before they vote

A 5-minute intro before you hand out the form. The day-one lesson plan covers this in more detail, but the short version:

1. We're building a class AI together. It'll have a name and a personality.
2. You're choosing the name and one or two personality traits.
3. The AI won't talk to you directly. It helps me make better stuff for you (slides, cards, etc.).
4. Your vote actually matters — I'm not asking you to validate something I already picked.
5. Be thoughtful with names. We're going to be saying this name a lot.

---

## The form (copy into Google Forms / paper / whatever you use)

---

### Page 1 — Intro

**Title:** Help name our class AI

**Description:**
You're voting on the name, personality, and mission of the AI assistant we'll be using this [quarter/semester]. Your input is real — I'll use your top choices. Take it seriously. Take it fun. Both.

---

### Question 1 — Name suggestions

**Question:** What should our class AI be called?

**Type:** Short answer (free text)

**Description (optional):** Give one suggestion. Pick something easy to say. Avoid copyrighted names (no Pokémon, no celebrities, no brands). Avoid jokes that won't be funny in three weeks. Think about what you'd want to hear on a slide or a card.

---

### Question 2 — Personality traits (pick a few)

**Question:** Which of these personality traits should the AI have? Pick up to three.

**Type:** Checkboxes (multiple selection)

**Options:**
- Warm — like a coach who notices the small wins
- Direct — gets to the point, doesn't waste your time
- Witty — finds the humor in things without being mean
- Curious — asks good questions, doesn't pretend to know everything
- Calm — steady, doesn't add to the noise
- Encouraging — believes you can do hard things
- Smart but humble — knows stuff but isn't a show-off
- Real — talks like a person, not a robot
- Stubbornly hopeful — sees the comeback before you do
- [Add or remove traits to fit your class]

---

### Question 3 — Voice samples (which one sounds most like our AI?)

**Question:** Read these three sample messages. Which one sounds most like the AI you want?

**Type:** Multiple choice (pick one)

**Options:**

**A.** "Hey — saw you turned in the lab today. That's three things this week. Keep that pace and you're going to be in a different place by Friday."

**B.** "Quick note. Lab in. Three things this week. You're moving. Don't stop now."

**C.** "Hey friend! Just popping in to celebrate something cool — you submitted the lab today, which makes THREE assignments this week! 🎉 You're absolutely crushing it! Keep that momentum!!"

*(A is warm and specific. B is direct and clipped. C is over-enthusiastic. Different classes want different things. There's no wrong answer — but C is included so you can see what most students will reject.)*

---

### Question 4 — Mission (what should the AI focus on?)

**Question:** What's the one thing you'd most want the AI to help with this [quarter/semester]?

**Type:** Multiple choice (pick one) — OR — short answer

**Options (if multiple choice):**
- Help me stay on top of missing assignments
- Help me prepare for tests
- Help me get unstuck when I don't know how to start
- Help me feel more recognized for the work I do
- Help me reflect on what I'm learning
- Other (write in below)

---

### Question 5 — What the AI should never do (write-in)

**Question:** What's one thing you'd want the AI to NEVER do? (one sentence)

**Type:** Short answer

**Description:** Examples: "Never embarrass anyone in front of the class." "Never use the word 'easy.'" "Never make assumptions about home life." You can pass on this if nothing comes to mind.

---

### Question 6 — Anything else (optional)

**Question:** Anything else you want me to know about this experiment?

**Type:** Long answer

**Description:** Concerns, ideas, hopes. Optional. Anonymous if you leave your name off.

---

### (Optional) Final question — Your name

**Question:** Your name (optional)

**Type:** Short answer

**Description:** Only fill this in if you want me to know who said what. The vote results are anonymous either way; this just lets me follow up with you if you wrote something I want to ask about.

---

## After the vote — what to do with the results

1. **Tally the name suggestions.** Pick the top 3–5 (consider blending if two are close).
2. **Run a runoff vote.** Same form, but the only question is "pick one of these finalists." Takes 2 minutes in class.
3. **Tally the personality traits.** Pick the top 2–3. These shape the AI's voice.
4. **Read the voice samples.** Use the most-picked one as a starting point for how the AI talks.
5. **Read the "what the AI should never do" responses.** Add the recurring ones to the "What [AI NAME] will never do" section in `brain/your-classroom-ai.md`.
6. **Update `brain/your-classroom-ai.md`** with what students chose.
7. **Open Cowork**, say hi, ask the AI to introduce itself in its new voice. Read its first message to your class.

If the kids' first reaction is "that doesn't sound right" — adjust the voice description and try again. They'll know within 10 seconds whether you got it.

---

## A note for the teacher

- **Some students will try to troll the name vote.** That's normal. Either screen suggestions before the runoff, or let one obviously-not-going-to-win troll name into the finalists so they feel heard. (Usually it loses.)
- **Don't promise to use the #1 vote no matter what.** Give yourself an out: "I'll go with what you pick unless there's a real problem (copyrighted, offensive, etc.). I'll explain if I have to override."
- **The voice samples question is the most valuable one.** Pay close attention to which one wins. If a class picks the "direct, clipped" style and the AI keeps sounding "warm and specific," the kids will tune out.
- **You can repeat the voice sample question mid-semester.** As the AI evolves with your class, run a quick 30-second check-in: "Which version of the AI's voice is working for you now?" Adjust accordingly.
